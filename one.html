What Is Managu?

The kikuyu term for the African nightshade plant is managu. It is known as Mnavu in Swahili, Namasaka to the Luhya, Osuga to the Luo, kitulu to the Kambas, and isoiyot to the Kipsigis. The flowery plant often has numerous leafy branches and reaches a height of around 1 meter. This vegetable is resilient, meaning it needs little to no upkeep to survive, and it is native to the weather of Africa. The seeds may be sown during a wet season, and they can be harvested in as little as 45 days. The top leaves of each shoot are then plucked off (harvested), allowing the remaining leaves to develop into larger, healthier leaves. Over the course of three months, you may do this repeatedly (once every week), earning great prices with each.

How Profitable Is It?

Every week, a 50*100 piece farm may yield between 200Kgs and 300Kgs of fresh managu. Ksh30 is the average price per kg. This implies you may earn at least Ksh6,000 per week or Ksh24,000 per month. The benefits are considerably better if you expand your farm's land. An acre of land, for example, may yield managus valued over Ksh.140,000 per month. Each harvesting season lasts three months, increasing your annual revenue to Ksh.420,000. A year has three seasons, thus a year of consistency can net you Ksh 1.26 million.

Challenges
